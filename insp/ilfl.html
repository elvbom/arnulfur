About the tour

The famous two hour walking tour focuses on the history of Iceland, the evolution of Reykjavík as a town and Icelandic culture in general, laid out in an informative and comic way by Marteinn (Martin) an english speaking Icelander and a history graduate. This tour has no fixed price, instead it runs on the great free walking tour model found allover the world where each participant sets the price of the tour afterwards by a tip. In other words, the tour runs completely on donations and as a result, the guide relies solely on your contribution.

About your guide

Who's this guy? My Citywalk enterprise

Citywalk was launched by me, Marteinn Briem (Martin), on may 7th 2014 and I‘m still the main guide. With increased popularity and especially in the summer you‘ll find a couple of additions in the Citywalk squad. Eiríkur (Eric) a good trusted friend, backpacker, motorcyclist, footballer and a history graduate was my first addition in march 2015. If you want to read an interesting cindarella story on how a momma‘s boy came to be the most famous guide in Iceland and how my Citywalk enterprise has evolved into being one of Iceland‘s leading tourism companies, read the history of Citywalk Ltd. here.
Today, Citywalk Ltd. offers various kinds of services. We have experience with luxury private tours in and out of the city, hikes outside Reykjavík, driving offroad on gravel and ice and even horseback riding tours. If you think we are the ones you want to organize your day trip, get in touch via the form below. My City Walk is expanding, but yet will always hold on to the original classic free walking tour route and the style.
Our latest addition is the Pubcrawl, since may 2015. Everyone is welcome to join the barhopping tour, we pick the bars, you show up and spend as little or as much as you like. We’ve secured deals on the bars and the idea is that the price paid for the pub crawl comes back to you in discounted drinks. Great company is guaranteed also! Read more on the pubcrawl page here!.
This summer (2015) we are also adding “Walk the Crash”, a guided tour through the causes and consequences of the collapse of the banking system in 2008. Magnús, an economic historian and expert in Icelandic financial history, will take you on a tour of some of the sights of the “financial miracle” of Iceland and the “pots and pans revolution” of 2008-9. Learn how and why Reykjavík aspired to become a global financial center, why the banks collapsed and why Iceland ended up jailing some of the bankers who were involved.
